My Work History

Over the past several years, I've held a variety of part-time jobs that have significantly shaped my understanding of workplace responsibility, strong customer service, and the importance of teamwork. Starting from a young age, I learned how to manage my time, stay organized, and maintain a strong work ethic while balancing school and job commitments. In each position, I adapted to different environments—whether that meant handling customer concerns at the front counter, preparing food quickly and accurately during rush hours, or keeping things running smoothly behind the scenes. These real-world experiences taught me how to stay calm under pressure, communicate clearly with both customers and coworkers, and problem-solve on the fly. More importantly, they instilled in me the confidence and professionalism needed in any job. Together, these experiences have laid a strong foundation for me to transition into more technical and creative roles—especially in the field of web development, which I am now pursuing.

Each job I’ve held provided me with unique skills and challenges that have helped me grow both personally and professionally. For example, working the early morning shifts at Country Donuts taught me how to be dependable and energized even before sunrise, preparing orders efficiently while maintaining a friendly attitude for every customer. At Portillo’s, a high-volume and fast-paced restaurant, I had to juggle multiple responsibilities at once—taking orders, preparing food, and ensuring accuracy under pressure during the dinner rush. These demanding environments taught me how to prioritize tasks, adapt quickly, and remain calm when things got hectic. I also worked at Domino’s, where I gained insight into delivery operations and inventory while assisting both in the kitchen and at the register. The combination of customer-facing duties and behind-the-scenes responsibilities has developed my time management, accountability, and clear communication skills—qualities that now support my ability to take on complex, detail-oriented web development projects.
