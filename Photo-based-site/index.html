About Me

I have been working consistently since I was 15 years old, gaining valuable experience across various fast-paced environments. My first job was at Jewel-Osco, where I started as a bagger and quickly learned the importance of customer service, teamwork, and efficiency. I was responsible for keeping the front end organized, assisting customers with groceries, and supporting the cashiers during busy hours. Over time, I grew comfortable interacting with all kinds of people and developed a strong work ethic rooted in reliability and punctuality. From there, I transitioned to working at Country Donuts, a local favorite in my area. This job taught me the value of small business operations and what it takes to keep customers coming back. I worked early morning shifts, often opening the store and handling the first wave of coffee and breakfast orders. It was a fast-moving job that demanded multitasking, a positive attitude, and attention to detail when serving both walk-in and drive-thru customers. Later, I joined Portillo’s, one of the busiest restaurants in town. Working in both the kitchen and on front-of-house tasks, I learned to handle pressure during rushes while still delivering consistent quality. The kitchen environment especially taught me discipline, communication, and how to function as part of a team. I also worked at Domino’s as a crew member, where I helped prepare pizzas, worked the register, and managed deliveries. These combined roles gave me well-rounded experience in food prep, order accuracy, and dealing with high-volume service.

This website is a showcase of the web development skills that I have learned.
